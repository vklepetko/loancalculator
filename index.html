How much can you borrow?

At the core it is about your ability to set money aside each month for regular payments. About one third to one half of your disposable income is good first guess of what lenders may offer you.


The way to borrow more money is to improve your financial standing, opting for longer term loans or finding a deal with lower interest rate. Be aware that the longer the term the more money you pay in interest.


In banker's jargon 'principal' is the term for the money one borrows from a lender.

How much is monthly payment?

The more money you want to borrow, the higher your monthly payments will have to be.


One can decrease monthly payment amount by either borrowing less, increasing count of monthly payments or finding a loan with lower interest rate.


Regular monthly payment is reffered to as 'annuity' in banker's jargon.

How expensive is the loan?

Interest rate tells you how expensive it is to keep unpaid debt. Interest rate is to a loan what fuel consumption is to a car. The higher it is the more expensive your journey gets. It may not bother you if you only use the car occasionally for short distances, but if you use the car for long term commute the costs start to compound quickly.


To minimize interest rate, do your market research and see offers from multiple lenders. To open better opportunities, maintain good credit history and cooperate with trustworhty lenders.

How long to keep paying?

The more money you borrowed and the lesser your monthly payment is the longer you will have to pay. Higher interest rates will also keep you paying longer.


If you goal is to pay as little of extra money as possible, try to get the term short. To do so you will either need to borrow less, borrow with lower interest rate or increase you monthly payment amount.


In banker's jargon, how long you will pay for debt is refferet to as the 'term' of the loan.
